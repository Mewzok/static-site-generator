# On Keeping Things Simple

Every project I've worked on has had a moment where I almost made it more complicated than it needed to be.

## The Temptation

It usually sounds like: "while I'm at it, I should also add..." That phrase has caused more bloated, half-finished code than anything else I know.

## What I Try to Do Instead

Build the simplest version that actually solves the problem. Ship it. Use it. Then add things only when the absence of them is genuinely painful.

## Why It Works

Simple things are easier to debug, easier to explain, and easier to come back to. A 100-line script you understand beats a 500-line framework you don't.
